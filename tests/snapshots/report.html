Methodology

Each ~100 ms frame of audio is read into memory, reduced to a single root-mean-square level in dBFS, and then discarded. A noise event is recorded when the level stays at or above -35 dBFS for at least 0.4 s; brief dips shorter than the 1.0 s debounce do not split one event into many. For each event we store start time, duration, and peak and average level — six numbers, no audio. No calibration offset is applied (Uncalibrated: levels are relative dBFS, not absolute SPL.).

Limitations

Levels are measured in dBFS, which is relative to digital full scale, not absolute sound pressure level (SPL) in dB. Without calibration against a reference meter, treat the numbers as relative, not absolute.

This tool measures sound levels only. It cannot prove what made a sound or where it came from; it does not record or identify any voice or source.

Microphone placement and room acoustics affect every reading; an event count reflects this device in this spot, not an absolute fact about the building. These numbers are offered to inform, not to manufacture a case.
